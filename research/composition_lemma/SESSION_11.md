### 2.2 Why "perfect but not semisimple"?

Это парадокс на первый взгляд: D^4 = [D^4, D^4] (perfect, Session 20.1 Theorem 21.1) BUT has solvable radical dim 145 (more than half!).

Resolution:
- "Perfect" means D^4 is generated by its own commutators.
- This compatible с large solvable radical IF the radical's "outside" generates more than itself via brackets с complement.
- Specifically: R ⊊ D^4, [D^4, R] = R (R is ideal), [D^4, D^4 \ R] generates rest.

It's a known phenomenon in modular Lie theory — "Frattini-type" behavior где algebra is perfect но not semisimple.
